Kreuzberg - our new home

When me and my friends and collegue Patricia first came here we were really lucky with getting an apartment. We actually got the first one we looked at. So unfortunatelly I don't have alot of experience looking around... although since it went so well for us I think my advice can be good anyway.

First week

I wouldn't recommend signing anything if you'are not in Berlin. Who knows what you might end up with. We started off by renting an apartment through Airbnb for one week in Kreuzberg where we wanted to live. That gave us some time to look around without feeling stressed. This was a great way of feeling the vibe in the neighbourhood.

Choose neighbourhood

First of all you choose where you want to live. From two mothns experience, this is my image of the popular neighbourhoods in Berlin:

  • Kreuzberg - one of the most popular places for people at the age of 18-32. It has a trashy and hip vibe to it with alot of streetart. It use to be really cheap but it has been up and coming for a while now. Lot's of cafés, restaurants, second hand shops and bars.
  • Neukölln - on the border to Kreuzberg. I think of it as the baby sister to Kreuzberg, it's not as expensive but it has the same vibe and will be up and coming. So this is a great place to invest in an apartment.
  • Prenzlauer Berg - a bit more clean and mature crowd than Kreuzberg. It still has a cool vibe, but the food and drinks are a bit more expensive. You often see some new parents in this neighbourhood.
  • Freidrichhain - a vibe that is in between Kreuzberg and Prezlauer. It has many cosy bars, shops and restaurats, but is not as busy as Kreuzberg. One of my favourite areas.
  • Mitte - the city center and of course close to everything. On the downside it's a bit harder to find charming places here.

Where to look?

We basically looked at every websites and sent a letter to each and everyone. It doesn't hurt to seem eager and show some interest. There's a mix of people who sublet their apartment.

Make a good impression

All they want to know is that you are really interested, wont make a mess and have money to pay the rent. Everything else doesn't really matter. So when the viewing occurs, just try to seem as seriouse, interested and positive as possible. As long as you don't sign anything you can always turn it down afterwords. Be on time! It's important to show that you can take responsibility. After the viewing, ask for an e-mail where you can send references, passport copies and proof of your salery. Check your spelling in the e-mail.
